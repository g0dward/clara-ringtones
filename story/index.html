The brief

For the trades and small teams Clara answers for, a ringing phone is money and a missed call is a lost job. So these tones have one job with two halves: cut through a loud van or a busy shop, and still be something you would happily hear twenty times a day. Not music to relax to. Not an alarm you come to dread. A call worth answering.

What shaped them

Motion makes the ring

The memorable tones never sit still: four to nine notes a second. Pause to breathe and it turns into a notification chime, not a call.

Steal the mechanism, not the melody

We took apart the tones people already know (Nokia, the old Apple marimba, the Slack call sounds) and kept what makes them work, not their notes.

Urgent, never alarming

The loud ones cut through with brightness and pace, not harshness. An alarm you would silence forever is a failed alarm.

Named for how they feel

Clara's voices are people: Grace, Connor, Rae. So the ringtones are moods instead: Hush, Sway, Pulse.
